Until now

After being diagnosed with leukemia from the radiation, Sadako spent her time in a nursing home folding origami paper cranes in hope of making a thousand of them. She was inspired to do so by the Japanese legend that one who created a thousand origami cranes would be granted a wish.

Sadako's story.

This is a story about Sadako Sasaki and the 1000 paper cranes.

When Sadako was born.

One day in 1943,they got a baby and named her Sadako Sasaki.She was one of the survivers in the explosion.

The paper crane journey.

When Sadako was in bed,one of her friends told her a Japanese legend said that if you fold a 1000 paper cranes,you will be granted a wish.

Started folding.

She started folding the paper cranes one by one.She folded a ton of paper cranes!!!

Until 1955.

She only managed to fold 655 cranes before she died of lukemia.All of her friends folded the rest on the cranes and made a mountument in Hiroshima,Japan.She died one morning in October 25th 1955.
